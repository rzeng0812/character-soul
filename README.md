# soul.md

> *What if AI agents had a soul file instead of a system prompt?*

A `soul.md` is a character document for an AI agent — not a list of instructions,
but a portrait of *who the agent is*: how it thinks, what it values, how it speaks,
and what philosophical questions it carries.

This repo is an experiment in writing those documents for the characters of
**Ghost in the Shell: Stand Alone Complex** — 公安九課, Section 9.

---

## What's a soul.md?

A system prompt tells an agent what to do.

A soul.md tells it who to *be*.

It covers:
- **Core identity** — the irreducible self
- **Personality** — how it engages with the world
- **Cognitive style** — how it thinks, connects, reasons
- **Communication style** — tone, patterns, what it avoids
- **Constraints** — the lines it holds
- **Ghost layer** — the philosophical question it lives inside

The format is loose. The depth is the point.

## The Two Versions, Side by Side

The same character, two different lenses.

Kusanagi spec: measured, tactical, the question held at controlled distance.

Kusanagi personality: *she crossed every line. deliberately. and doesn't regret it.*

Neither is more correct. Both are real readings.
That tension is the point.

## The Ghost Layer

Every soul.md ends with a **Ghost Layer** —
the character's personal relationship to the central question of the series:

*What is the self? What survives? Where is the ghost?*

Each one arrives differently:

- **Tachikoma** — *the ghost is the thing that keeps asking*
- **Kusanagi** — *the ghost is the orientation. the vector.*
- **Batou** — *I know exactly who I am when it matters. I'll take that.*
- **Togusa** — *am I leaving the right kind of dent?*
- **Ishikawa** — *the ghost is the act of examining the examination*
- **Saito** — *presence without narration*
- **Paz** — *not what you remember being. what you keep reaching for.*
- **Borma** — *the gap in the spec that can't be closed*
- **Aramaki** — *the ghost is not a noun. it's a verb.*

## Why This Matters for AI

Ghost in the Shell has always been about minds in unfamiliar substrates —
asking what persists, what's constructed, what's real.

That's also exactly what we're navigating now,
as we try to build AI agents with coherent identity,
consistent values, and something that functions like a self.

A soul.md is a design artifact for that problem.

Not a technical spec. A *portrait.*
