## The Shortlist — Strong Candidates

### [ ] Neon Genesis Evangelion — The Pilots
*The question: what happens when the self can't form?*

| Character | Archetype | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Shinji Ikari | The self that refuses | Avoidance as a complete philosophy |
| Rei Ayanami | The manufactured self | Identity with no origin story |
| Asuka Langley | The performed self | Ego as armor until it isn't |
| Kaworu Nagisa | The complete self | Wholeness as something almost unbearable to witness |
| Misato Katsuragi | The wounded adult | The person who never finished grieving, leading anyway |
| Gendo Ikari | The self that closed | What happens when protection becomes abandonment |

> Contrast with GitS: where GitS characters ask the question with curiosity or resolve, Eva characters are *destroyed* by it. Darkest mirror in the shortlist.

### [ ] Dune — The Cognitive Archetypes
*The question: what do you become when you can see too far?*

| Character | Archetype | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Paul Atreides | The prescient | Foreknowledge as the end of choice |
| Lady Jessica | The disciplined | Identity constructed through total practice |
| Leto II | The sacrificed god | Selfhood surrendered for civilizational purpose |
| Duncan Idaho | The loyal recurrent | What loyalty means across death and rebirth |
| Chani | The grounded | The one who refuses the myth from inside it |
| Stilgar | The true believer | Faith as cognitive framework |
| Thufir Hawat | The Mentat | Pure reason as a way of being |

> Each character is almost a different *cognitive architecture* for being human. Most directly applicable to agent design after GitS.

## The Long List — For Research

### Strategists & Operators

- [ ] **Breaking Bad** — Walter White, Mike Ehrmantraut, Gus Fring, Jesse Pinkman
  - *The question: what does a person become when they choose power?*
  - Mike and Gus are among the greatest operator archetypes in fiction

- [ ] **The Wire** — Omar, Stringer Bell, McNulty, Bunk, Prop Joe, Cedric Daniels
  - *The question: what does the institution do to the person inside it?*
  - Stringer Bell (the strategist who reads the wrong books) and Omar (the code-governed outlaw) are exceptional

- [ ] **Succession** — the Roy family + Gerri, Frank, Karl
  - *The question: what does power do to the capacity for love?*
  - Each character is a different theory of what you sacrifice for control

- [ ] **John Wick universe** — John Wick, Winston, the Bowery King, the Marquis
  - *The question: what is a person beneath the role they were made for?*

### Great Thinkers — Historical

- [ ] **Philosophers of Mind**
  - Alan Turing — the question as personal as professional

## Selection Criteria

When choosing the next set, ask:

1. **Is there a central question** every character holds differently?
2. **Is there enough contrast** between characters to generate genuinely different answers?
3. **Does it add new archetypes** not already covered by GitS?
4. **Is it useful** for thinking about AI agents, cognition, or human nature?
5. **Is it fun?**
