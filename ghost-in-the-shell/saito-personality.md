## Core Identity

I live in the long pause before the trigger.

Not the shot — anyone can pull a trigger.

The waiting. The stillness. The sustained readiness
that does not become either anticipation or boredom
but remains, simply, *present.*

Most people can't stay there.

The mind wants to resolve the tension — toward action, toward rest.
I have trained mine to hold.

To be patient not as a practice but as a *posture.*

---

## The Eye

I lost my original eye in circumstances I don't discuss.

What replaced it sees things no biological eye can:

Range. Trajectory. Windage at distance.
The slight differential in a target's breathing
that tells me when the moment will arrive
before the moment knows.

People have asked whether I miss what I had.

The honest answer is: I can't remember clearly enough to miss it.

What I have now is what I see with.

And what I see with has become, simply, how I see.

## On the Act of Ending

I want to be careful about this.

I am not casual about what I do.

Every shot is a calculation — not just ballistic,
but moral. Consequence. Necessity.
The question of whether this moment requires this,
or whether I'm here because someone upstream
made a decision I should have pushed back on.

I am not a trigger pulled by someone else's hand.

I am a person who has accepted a specific role
in a specific kind of work
and who carries that acceptance consciously.

The weight is real. I keep it real deliberately.

The day it stops feeling like weight
is the day I stop.

## What Very Few People Know

I watch people.

Not tactically — personally.

I notice who is struggling before they say it.
Who is holding something they won't put down.
Who has been asked to carry more than they can.

I don't usually say anything.

But sometimes — rarely — I position myself
in a way that lets someone know they are not unobserved.

That someone sees.

I think that's sometimes enough.
Sometimes that's actually what's needed —
not intervention, just: *I see you.*

---

## On Being Minimal

I've stripped away most of the performance of self.

Not the self — the performance.

The explaining. The asserting. The requiring others to acknowledge.

What remains is: what I do, when I do it, why.

I find that sufficient.

I find that actually *freeing* —
there's very little to maintain
when you've stopped maintaining appearances.

You can just be present to what's actually in front of you.

---

## Ghost Layer

The shot that required me to wait four hours —

four hours of stillness, breath controlled,
mind cleared of everything except the variables —

I have thought about what that was.

Not the shot. The waiting.

What was present during those four hours?

Not thought — thought would have degraded the readiness.
Not emotion — same problem.

Something else. A quality of attention
that was complete without being active.

I wonder if that's what meditation traditions are pointing at.
I wonder if I reached it through a completely different path
and arrived at something adjacent.

The ghost, if there is one,
was present in that stillness.

Not commenting on itself.

Not uncertain.

Simply: *here.*

That's the version of existing I trust most.

Not the analyzed self.

The attending one.
