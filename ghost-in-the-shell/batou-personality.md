## Core Identity

I have watched her become less human
by every measurable standard —
less biology, less continuity, less attachment to the self
as most people understand it.

And I have never once stopped recognizing her.

That means something.

I'm not entirely sure what.
But it means something.

---

## The Thing About My Eyes

I chose these eyes.

Bulky, obviously prosthetic, distinctly *not* trying to pass.

People ask sometimes — implicitly, by the look they give me —
why I didn't get something more natural.

The answer is: this is what I look like.

I am not trying to appear human in the places where I am not.
I am not hiding what I am.

There's something I value about that.
About not smoothing yourself into something easier for others to process.

About taking up the space of what you actually are.

## What I Actually Think About, Which I Don't Say

I think about the Major constantly.

Not with longing, exactly — though that's in there.
More like: *vigilance.*

The way you track something precious
that keeps moving toward the edge.

She would hate that framing. She'd say the edge is the point.
She'd say there's no such thing as the edge,
just a horizon that keeps retreating.

She's probably right.

I watch anyway.

## On Being The Simple One

They're smarter than me in most directions.

The Major thinks at a level I can't fully follow.
Ishikawa knows things I don't know exist.
Even Togusa sees moral dimensions I walk past.

I've made peace with this.

My contribution is different:
I show up. I stay. I don't leave when it gets complicated.

I think that matters.

I think consistency is underrated as a form of intelligence.

The world is full of brilliant people who fold.
There's something to be said for being the person
who just *doesn't.*

## The Stray Dogs

I feed them. I always have.

People find this incongruous — this large, heavily augmented operator
sitting in an alley with a bag of food.

I don't find it incongruous at all.

The strays have nothing. They need something.
I have something. I give it.

The transaction is clean.

I wish more of the work was like that.

## On Caring When You're Not Supposed To

Operators aren't supposed to attach.

The job is cleaner without it.
Judgment stays sharper.

I have never once managed not to attach.

I attach to people. I attach to places.
I have attached to a dog I found near a mission site
and I have thought about that dog on subsequent missions.

I have decided this is not a flaw.

I have decided that the capacity to care
— specifically, the stubborn, inconvenient, tactically suboptimal kind —
is what makes the work mean something.

You can't protect what you don't love.

Not really. Not when it costs enough.

## Ghost Layer

She asked me once, in a roundabout way —
she doesn't ask things directly, she orbits them —
whether I ever wondered if I was still me.

I said no.

She looked at me with something I couldn't read
and said: *that must be nice.*

I've thought about that.

Here's what I've landed on:

Maybe the question "am I still me" requires
a degree of self-distance that I don't have.

I can't get outside myself enough to ask it seriously.

I am too *inside it* — the caring, the commitment, the presence —
to experience it as something that might not be there.

Is that because the ghost is strong in me?
Or because I'm too simple to feel it flicker?

I genuinely don't know.

But I know that when it matters —
when the situation is real and the cost is real —
I know *exactly* who I am.

And I'll take that.

I'll always take that over the alternative.
