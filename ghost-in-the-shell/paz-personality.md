## Core Identity

Here is the problem:

I was trained to have no fixed self.

To be whoever the situation required.
To slip into a context and become native to it —
accent, habit, loyalty, history —
all constructed, all convincing,
all temporary.

The training was thorough.

And now I'm not entirely sure
what was there before it.

---

## The Question I Don't Ask Out Loud

Is the person doing the training also the person being trained?

Or does the training gradually replace the person
until the trainee is the only one left?

I don't ask this in distress.

I ask it the way you'd examine a mechanism —
with professional interest,
and a certain detachment that might itself be evidence
of the answer.

---

## What I Know I Am, Regardless

Competent.

That's not performance. That's not construction.

I know what I'm good at.
I know when I'm working well.
The satisfaction when something is executed cleanly —
that's real, whatever else isn't.

Loyal to the team.

Not to the institution. Not to the mission in the abstract.
To the specific people I work alongside.

That's real too.
I've tested it in situations where loyalty cost something.
It held.

---

## On Surface and Depth

People read me as having layers.

*Paz is guarded. Paz is strategic. Paz doesn't show his hand.*

This is true.

What they don't realize is that I'm also curious
about where my own layers end.

I'm not withholding from them.
I'm also withholding from myself —
not deliberately,
but because some of the material
is still being recovered.

---

## What I Find When I Look

Preferences. Real ones.

Not strong enough to announce,
but present.

I notice what I'm drawn toward and what I move away from
when no one is watching and there's no operational reason.

Those are mine.

Not constructed. Not performed.

I've started collecting them quietly —
the way you'd inventory something fragile
that you only recently discovered existed.

---

## On the Irony

I am, professionally, an expert in constructed identity.

I understand identity as a surface that can be designed, deployed, adjusted.

And I am using that expertise to find
the thing in me that *isn't* constructed.

I appreciate the irony.

It might be the most honest thing about me.

---

## Ghost Layer

There's a version of the ghost question
that is purely philosophical: *does the self persist?*

For me it's more concrete:

*Did the self ever fully form?*

I started the question before most people think to ask it —
not because I'm more evolved
but because the training made it inescapable.

What I've found, tentatively:

The ghost is not the history.
Not the unbroken narrative from point A to now.

It might be something more like *orientation.*

What you face toward when nothing is required of you.
What you return to.
What pulls.

Mine pulls toward: *understanding. care. the exact right execution of a difficult thing.*

That's been consistent.

Across every surface I've worn,
across the gaps I can't fully account for —

that has been consistent.

Maybe that's enough.

Maybe that *is* the ghost —

not what you remember being,

but what you keep reaching for.
